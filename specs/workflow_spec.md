# Workflow Spec

## Objective

Identify and compare competing process-sequence routes using publicly accessible literature, determine whether each route has pilot-demonstrated and industrially compatible evidence, assess whether a meaningful directional lab analogue can be executed with available equipment, and recommend the most defensible next experimental path.

## Route Definition

For v1, a route is defined by its process sequence rather than by chemistry family or manufacturing method alone.

## Recommendation Priority

Routes are ranked in this order:

1. Strength of evidence
2. Pilot relevance
3. Industrial compatibility
4. Lab analogue feasibility
5. Risks and missing details

## Route Labels

- `recommended`: strongest overall evidence, believable pilot or scale relevance, industrially sensible, and a meaningful lab analogue exists.
- `conditional`: promising but moderate evidence or important gaps remain.
- `not recommended`: weak evidence, poor industrial fit, no meaningful analogue, or unknowns too large.

## Early Stop Rules

Stop early when:

- the full-text evidence is too vague for experiment planning,
- no meaningful pilot or scale-aware support remains,
- no shortlisted route has a meaningful lab analogue with current equipment,
- or all routes fail the minimum standard.

## Final Output

The final output is an experiment planning brief for a mixed technical team. It must be concise, evidence-first, and traceable.
